Fun Facts

💡 Did You Know?

Benue State is officially nicknamed the Food Basket of the Nation because it produces the highest variety of staple crops in Nigeria.

💡 Did You Know?

Cocoa House in Ibadan, once the tallest building in tropical Africa, was built entirely from the proceeds of Cocoa exports in the 1960s.

💡 Did You Know?

Michael Ibru pioneered the frozen fish business in Nigeria in 1957. Before his success, many Nigerians mockingly referred to it as 'mortuary fish.'

💡 Did You Know?

Ofada Rice is named after Ofada, a small community in Ogun State where this unique, short-grain indigenous rice was first cultivated.

💡 Did You Know?

Zaki Biam Yam Market in Benue State is the largest mono-product market in West Africa, dedicated almost exclusively to yams.

💡 Did You Know?

Onitsha Main Market in Anambra State is often cited as the largest market in Africa by both geographical size and the volume of goods traded.

💡 Did You Know?

Ginger from Nigeria (specifically from Kaduna State) is considered among the best in the world due to its high pungency and oil content.

💡 Did You Know?

Nearly 50 percent of all fruits and vegetables produced in Nigeria are lost to post-harvest loss before they ever reach the consumer, mainly due to poor storage.

💡 Did You Know?

Jollof Rice actually originated from the Wolof people of Senegal and Gambia, but has become Nigeria’s most famous unofficial national dish.

💡 Did You Know?

Honey Beans (Oloyin) are unique to the Nigerian region and are prized specifically for their natural sweet taste compared to other brown beans.

💡 Did You Know?

Nigeria is the world's largest consumer of Rice in Africa, but also one of its largest producers thanks to the massive expansion of local mills in the last decade.

Factors Impacting Prices

Harvest seasonality & local supply — price declines as harvests arrive.

High but easing food inflation — elevated food inflation through 2025 with a downward trend.

FX / policy & import dependence — imported staples such as rice are sensitive to naira movements and trade policy.

Fuel, transport & input costs — fuel and fertilizer price volatility raises costs.

Conflict & localized disruption — areas with insecurity tend to see price spikes, increasing regional uncertainity.
